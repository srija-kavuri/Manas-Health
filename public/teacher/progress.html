Understanding Mental Health Results

Student mental health results are presented in three categories: Normal, Mild, and High. Each category provides insights into different aspects of student well-being. Here's what each category signifies:

Normal:

Normal results suggest that student mental health falls within typical ranges, indicating a balanced and healthy state. Congratulations on maintaining a positive well-being! Continue practicing self-care and maintaining healthy habits to sustain student mental wellness.

Mild:

Mild results indicate a lower level of concern or symptoms. While not severe, it's essential to pay attention to these areas and consider proactive steps for improvement. Explore strategies for stress reduction, self-care, and positive coping mechanisms to enhance student overall mental health.

Moderate:

A "Moderate" result signals a noticeable impact on student mental health. It's essential to pay attention to these aspects and explore support systems or professional guidance for further assistance.

Severe:

An "Extremely Severe" result highlights critical concerns requiring urgent attention. Reach out to mental health professionals, friends, or family for support and guidance. student well-being is a top priority.

Extremely Severe:

An "Extremely Severe" result indicates a critical and urgent need for intervention. This category suggests severe and pervasive symptoms that may significantly impact daily functioning. It's crucial to seek immediate professional help and support from mental health experts, friends, or family.

Remember, these categories provide a general framework, and individual experiences may vary. Use this information to guide student self-reflection and consider consulting with mental health experts for personalized insights and assistance.
